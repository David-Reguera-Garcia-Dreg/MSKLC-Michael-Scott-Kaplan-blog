There is no more Limonata at Trader Joe's

by Michael S. Kaplan, published on 2007/05/13 03:01 -04:00, original URI: http://blogs.msdn.com/b/michkap/archive/2007/05/13/2589142.aspx

(This is a post about Limonata)

 

Yesterday afternoon, I scooted over to Trader Joe's to get a few cases of San Pellegrino Limonata. I'm down to just a few and I like to stay stocked up, after all.

The shelf had nothing. And no space for it either.

I look around for an employee to ask. She checks with the folks at the front desk, and it turns out that Trader Joe's isn't carrying it anymore.

"Does not sell enough units," apparently. Though she pointed out that was a never a problem in this particular store.

(She looked over and noticed I had three empty cardboard cases as she said that, and smiled.)

Damn.

I won't bother that text again like I did in this post and this other one. But as changes go this one kind of stinks.

I am floored in the hugely competitive Seattle grocery store market that this sort of thing would happen. Though in theory they may start carrying it again if they find a supplier to work with.

I also just realized the downside of buying in quantity -- it leaves you a few weeks behind the times when changes like this happen. :-(

I scooted back home and over to the car. Top down, I drove out to Larry's in Redmond, where the assistant grocery manager was floored. She wondered who their suppliers could be that they wouldn't be able to get cases of items that sell in quantity.

No matter, really -- as she will be happy to order it any time I need it. Trader Joe's loss is Larry's gain....

Though it does mean that when I head out to San Jose next week to go the Unicode Technical Committee meeting that I'll have to bring some Limonata with me myself (I verified that they have none left at the Mountain View store either).

Which means I'll have to check a bag -- TSA won't let you carry cans of anything through security these days.

Ah well, there are worse things than checking a bag....

Too bad it isn't an item that Microsoft stocks in the cafeterias; I could put in a Product Studio bug about it. :-)

At least I can still find some Limonata!
