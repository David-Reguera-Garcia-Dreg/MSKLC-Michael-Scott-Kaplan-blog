How to get home health care covered while you still go work every day

by Michael S. Kaplan, published on 2013/12/24 19:44 -08:00, original URI: http://sortingtherestallout.blogspot.com/2013/12/how-to-get-home-health-care-covered.html

It turns out to be a very complicated problem. Not for its own sake. But for the sake of the health insurance company and all of its rules. Now my health insurance is Premera Blue Cross Microsoft, which means it's Microsoft with Premera administrating the plan. But there are still rules to be followed. Like the requirements surrounding home health care (which I clearly need) which I had no problem getting when I was in Building 25. The rest of my group was in Building 86, but 86's inaccessibility last year was legendary. But after half a year and half a million dollars, Building 86 was completely accessible, not just for me but for everyone else working there. And to tell you the truth, it was just in time. Because a lot of being a Senior Program Manager in World Readiness requires real face time -- with champions, with team members, with management. For "influence without authority" to be successful, that face time is required. So now that the building is accessible, everything is good now, right? Well, almost. I have to go to work every day to get the job done. But going to work every day makes me ineligible for home health care. There is no justice to be had here. Or is there? My neurologist is recommending that I work from home. I know that I can't, so AMA (against medical advice), I have to keep going in.
